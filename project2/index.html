Proposition

Pro position: The global energy system is transitioning toward renewable energy

Opposing position: The global energy system has not really shifted to renewable energy because fossil fuels have not been replaced

Pro Visualizations

We are trying to persuade that the global energy system uses more and more renewable source to produce electricity. The first column graph shows that the percentage difference between the electricity produced from renewable sources and produced from unrenewable sources is getting smaller the second graph is a more direct line graph that shows the changes in the percentage of electricity produced by renewable source.

Opposing Visualizations

The graphs show changes in fossil fuel generation over time. The first graph shows a relatively stable trend by compressing the y-axis range, so that fossil fuel power generation changes little. Fossil fuel use has remained at the same level or even increased since 2000. The 2000 baseline is included to emphasize that electricity generation is currently higher than it has been in the past. The argument is that renewable energy is built on top of fossil fuels and is intended to supplement, not replace, fossil fuels. The graphs focus on the total amount of fossil fuels (in absolute terms) rather than proportional changes, giving the impression that fossil fuels are not being replaced.

Which side do I lean toward

We are leaning towards the opposing visualizations, it seems more convincing that fossil fuels will still be an important part of the global system in the future. This set of visualizations shows a clear trend that the use of fossil fuels have not decreased over the past 20 years. Therefore, it seems that fossil fuels will not be replaced by renewable energy. The other set of visualizations shows that renewable energy is used more widely than fossil fuels. However, they are not very strong in suggesting that renewable energy will replace fossil fuels. In the first plot, both renewable energy and fossil fuels have increased. The second graph shows that renewable energy is taking over a larger share of the total electricity generation. It used an interesting technique of tweaking the y-axis to amplify the increase of renewable energy; however, the plot is not particularly expressive.
